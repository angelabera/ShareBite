Join the ShareBite Network

We are building a hunger-free community by connecting those who have surplus food with those who need it. Whether you are an organization or an individual, you are the bridge that makes this possible.

How the Donation Cycle Works

1. Donors List Food

Restaurants, bakeries, and households list their surplus fresh food on ShareBite using their "Donor" account.

2. You Collect It

NGOs & Volunteers (You!) receive alerts, claim the listing, and pick up the food from the donor.

3. Feed the Community

The food is distributed to shelters, community centers, or individuals in need. Waste is reduced, smiles are created.

Why Join ShareBite?

Join a network that turns surplus into sustenance. We empower you with the tools to find food nearby and deliver it to those who need it most. Together, we make localized impact not just possible, but efficient and scalable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the food collection and distribution process work?

The process is designed to be simple and efficient. First, a donor (restaurant, bakery, or household) lists their surplus food on the ShareBite app, providing details like food type, quantity, and pickup time.

Once listed, nearby NGOs and registered Volunteers receive an instant notification. You can review the details and "Claim" the listing if you are available to pick it up. After claiming, you will get the donor's contact details to coordinate the pickup. Finally, you deliver the food to the nearest shelter or community center and mark the request as "Completed" in the app.

What are the safety and hygiene guidelines for donated food?

Safety is our top priority. All donors are required to certify that the food is fresh, untouched, and packed in clean containers. We strictly do not accept expired food, leftovers from plates, or food that has been unrefrigerated for too long.

As a volunteer or NGO, you should perform a visual and smell check upon pickup. If the food appears spoiled or unsafe, you have the right to reject the pickup and report the issue through the app. We also recommend using insulated bags for transport to maintain food temperature.

Do I need specific documents or qualifications to join as an NGO?

Yes, to ensure transparency and trust, organizations must provide valid registration documents. We require a copy of your NGO registration certificate (Trust, Society, or Section 8 Company), a valid PAN card of the organization, and proof of your registered office address.

For individual volunteers, no formal qualification is needed, but you must submit a valid government-issued ID (like Aadhar, Driving License, or Voter ID) for identity verification. Once submitted, our team verifies these documents within 24-48 hours before activating your account.

Can I volunteer if I don't have a vehicle or can only help on weekends?

Absolutely! We value every bit of help. If you do not have a vehicle, you can filter for pickups that are within walking distance or use public transport if feasible. You can also partner with another volunteer who has a vehicle.

Regarding availability, you are completely free to choose when you want to help. There are no mandatory hours. You can simply toggle your status to "Available" on weekends or evenings when you are free, and claim requests that fit your schedule.
